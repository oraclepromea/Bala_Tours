About Bala Tours

Certified company in sustainable tourism responsible practices

Bala Tours was founded 21 years ago with the goal of providing excellent, environmental and social responsible tourism services in the Bolivian Amazon.

We operate with a team of local well-trained and experienced guides born in the region. In order to guarantee the quality of our services, we work with small groups. You will not only see the natural beauties of this region, but also learn about the secrets of nature and about our history and traditions.

Bala Tours has been certified by the Municipal Government of Rurrenabaque for fulfilling sustainable tourism guidelines. These guidelines include environmental, social and responsible management standards.

What to Bring

Essential items for your Amazon adventure

Clothing & Protection

Essential gear for comfort and safety in the Amazon environment.

  • Long-sleeved shirts and pants (quick-dry)
  • Light rain jacket or poncho
  • Hat with brim and walking shoes
  • Waterproof sandals and swimwear
  • Insect repellent and sunscreen

Health & Safety

Important health items to ensure your comfort during the tour.

  • Personal first aid kit and medications
  • Sunglasses and biodegradable toiletries
  • Hand sanitizer for hygiene
  • Prescription medications you need
  • Motion sickness medication if needed

Equipment & Accessories

Recommended equipment to enhance your Amazon experience.

  • Camera with extra batteries and memory
  • Binoculars for wildlife observation
  • Flashlight or headlamp with batteries
  • Power bank and waterproof bags
  • Small backpack and quick-dry towel

Pro Tip: Pack light! Laundry service is available at both ecolodges.

Amazing Wildlife You'll Encounter

Experience the incredible biodiversity of the Bolivian Amazon

Pink River Dolphins

Swim with these amazing creatures in the pristine waters of Pampas del Yacuma. These intelligent mammals are unique to the Amazon basin and offer an unforgettable swimming experience.

Colorful Macaws

Observe the rare and endemic Blue-throated Macaw, found only in Bolivia. This endangered species is a highlight for birdwatchers and conservation enthusiasts.

Friendly Capybaras

Meet the world's largest rodents in their natural habitat. These gentle giants are often seen lounging by the riverbanks and swimming in the waters.

Spectacular Caimans

Spot these impressive reptiles during night excursions. Our guides will safely show you caimans in their natural environment along the riverbanks.

Howler Monkeys

Listen to the distinctive calls of howler monkeys echoing through the jungle canopy. These primates are among the loudest animals in the world.

Three-Toed Sloths

Discover these slow-moving marvels hanging in the rainforest canopy. Sloths are masters of camouflage and energy conservation.

Baby Monkeys with Families

Witness heartwarming moments of monkey families in their natural habitat. Baby monkeys learning from their mothers is one of the most touching wildlife experiences.

Vibrant Kingfishers

Watch these colorful birds dive for fish along the riverbanks. The Amazon is home to multiple species of these beautiful fishing birds.

Exotic Butterflies

Marvel at the incredible diversity of butterflies in the Amazon. From tiny jewel-like species to large morpho butterflies with brilliant blue wings.

Majestic Birds of Prey

Spot impressive hawks and other raptors soaring above the Amazon canopy. These skilled hunters are essential to the ecosystem's balance.

Legendary Piranhas

Try your hand at piranha fishing in the Amazon waters. Despite their reputation, these fish are fascinating creatures and surprisingly tasty when cooked!

Colorful Tree Frogs

Discover the amazing variety of frogs in the Amazon rainforest. From tiny poison dart frogs to larger tree frogs, each species is uniquely adapted to jungle life.

Ancient River Turtles

Encounter these prehistoric creatures sunning themselves on logs and riverbanks. Some turtle species in the Amazon can live for over 100 years.

Amazonian Tarantulas

For the brave-hearted, observe these impressive spiders in their natural habitat. Tarantulas play an important role in controlling insect populations.

Intelligent Capuchin Monkeys

Watch these clever primates use tools and solve problems in the wild. Capuchin monkeys are among the most intelligent animals in the Amazon.

Spectacular Exotic Birds

The Amazon is home to over 1,300 bird species. From tiny hummingbirds to magnificent toucans, the diversity is absolutely breathtaking.
